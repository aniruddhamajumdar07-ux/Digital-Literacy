# Stay Safe with UPI: How to Protect Yourself from Online Payment Frauds

UPI has made sending and receiving money very easy. But some people are using this convenience to cheat others.

Every day, many people across India lose money due to clever tricks through WhatsApp, SMS, calls, and fake links. Knowing these frauds can help you and your family stay protected.

## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI fraud occurs when scammers trick you into sending money directly to them or revealing your UPI PIN, OTP, or other banking details.

Once the money leaves your account, it quickly moves through multiple other accounts, making recovery very difficult and often impossible.

## Common Ways People Get Tricked

Fraudsters use several common methods:

- **The Friend or Family Emergency Scam**  
  Someone messages you pretending to be your friend, cousin, or relative. They use a similar profile picture and claim they are in trouble — usually a medical emergency or accident. They send a QR code or UPI ID and urge you to send money immediately. After the payment, they block you.

- **The Official Call Scam**  
  You receive a call from someone claiming to be from your bank, RBI, police, or a government office. They scare you by saying your account is at risk or that you are eligible for a refund. They then ask for your UPI PIN or OTP for "verification".  
  **Important**: No genuine bank or government official will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP over phone or message.

- **Dangerous Links and Fake Apps**  
  You get a message with a link saying “Check your refund”, “Claim cashback”, or similar offers. Clicking the link can install malware that steals your banking information silently. Some links show fake UPI screens that look exactly like the real app.

**Other common tricks** include fake “Request Money” notifications, asking you to download remote access apps, swapping QR codes at shops, or sending forged payment screenshots.

## Who Usually Falls for These Frauds?

- College students managing their own money for the first time
- Senior citizens who are not very familiar with smartphones
- People who are in a hurry or emotionally stressed
- Small shopkeepers and online sellers

## What Happens After Getting Scammed?

The impact is much more than just financial loss:

- You can lose thousands or even lakhs of rupees in seconds
- It causes severe stress, anxiety, and sometimes depression
- You start doubting even genuine friends and family members
- Recovering the money is slow and not always possible
- You end up wasting a lot of time following up with banks and police

## Real-Life Inspired Story: Rohan’s Mistake

Rohan was a first-year student in Bhopal. He had recently started using PhonePe and Google Pay for his daily expenses.

One evening, he received a WhatsApp message from an unsaved number. The profile picture looked exactly like his school friend Arjun. The message said Arjun had met with a bike accident and needed ₹7,500 urgently for medicines.

Feeling worried, Rohan quickly scanned the QR code and transferred the money using his UPI PIN.

Later that night, when he called Arjun on his saved number, he discovered it was a scam. Arjun was perfectly fine and had not sent any message.

Rohan had lost ₹7,500 to a stranger in just a few seconds. This incident taught him an important lesson. From that day, he always verifies urgent requests by calling the person on their known number and never scans unknown QR codes without double-checking.

## How to Stay Protected: Must-Follow Rules

- Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone — not even if they claim to be from the bank.
- Always verify before sending money in emergencies. Call the person using their saved contact number.
- Pause and think when someone creates urgency. Scammers often say “Do it fast!”
- Do not click on suspicious links or scan unknown QR codes.
- Set a reasonable daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app.
- Use only official UPI apps and keep them updated.
- Enable fingerprint or face lock on your UPI apps.
- For shopkeepers: Always confirm the payment in your own app before delivering goods.

## What to Do If You Have Been Scammed

Act immediately:

- Call your bank right away and request to block the transaction.
- Dial the National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**
- File a complaint on the official website: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- Change your UPI PIN immediately.
- Keep all screenshots, transaction details, and messages as proof.

The faster you report, the better your chances of stopping the fraud.

## Final Words

UPI is a powerful and convenient tool that has changed the way India makes payments. However, with convenience comes the responsibility of staying aware and cautious.

Be smart. Be careful. Always verify before you trust.

Share this information with your family and friends — especially students and parents. A little awareness today can save a lot of money tomorrow.

**Stay alert. Stay safe with UPI.**
