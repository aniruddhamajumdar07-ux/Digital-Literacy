# Stay Safe with UPI. How to Protect Yourself from Online Payment Frauds

UPI has made it very easy to send and receive money.. Some people are using this to cheat others.

Every day many people in India lose money because of tricks on WhatsApp, SMS calls and fake links. If you know about these tricks you can save yourself and your family from becoming victims.

## What Exactly is UPI Payment Fraud

UPI fraud happens when someone tricks you into sending money to them or telling them your UPI PIN, OTP or banking details.

Once the money leaves your account it goes through other accounts very quickly making it hard to get it back.

## Common Ways People Get Tricked

Here are some tricks used by fraudsters:

* The Friend or Family Emergency Scam: someone messages you pretending to be your friend, cousin or relative. They use a picture and say they are in trouble like a medical emergency. They send a QR code or UPI ID. Ask you to send money quickly. After you pay they. Block you.

* The Official Call Scam: you get a call from someone who says they are from your bank, RBI, police or government office. They scare you by saying your account is at risk or you are eligible for a refund. Then they ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. Remember, no real bank or government official will ask for your UPI PIN or OTP on the phone or in a message.

Dangerous Links and Fake Apps: you get a message with a link that says "Check your refund" or "Claim cashback". Clicking on the link can install software that steals your banking information without you knowing. Some links show UPI screens that look like the real app.

Other tricks include sending Request Money notifications asking you to download apps for help swapping QR codes at shops or sending fake payment screenshots.

## Who Usually Falls for These Frauds

Some people who usually fall for these tricks are:

* College students who are managing money on their own for the time

* Citizens who are not familiar with smartphones

* People who are in a hurry or stressed

* Small shopkeepers and online sellers

## What Happens After You Get Scammed

If you get scammed the damage is not just about losing money. You can lose a lot of money it causes stress, anxiety and sometimes depression. You start doubting your real friends and family. Recovering the money is slow and not possible. You waste time following up with banks and police.

## Real Life Inspired Story: Rohan's Mistake

Rohan was a student in Bhopal. He used PhonePe. Google Pay for his daily expenses. One evening he got a WhatsApp message from a number he did not save. The picture looked like his friend Arjun. The message said Arjun had a bike accident and needed 7500 rupees urgently. Rohan felt worried and quickly sent the money. Later he found out it was a scam. He had sent 7500 rupees to a stranger in a few seconds. This incident taught him a lesson. From that day Rohan always verifies requests by calling the person on their known number and never scans random QR codes without checking.

## How to Stay Protected: Must Follow Rules

* Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone not even if they claim to be from the bank.

* Always verify before sending money for emergencies. Call the person on their saved contact number.

*. Think when someone creates urgency. Scammers love to say "Do it fast".

* Do not click links or scan unknown QR codes.

* Set a daily UPI limit in your banking app for extra safety.

* Use official UPI apps and keep them updated.

* Enable fingerprint. Face lock on your UPI apps.

* For shopkeepers always check the payment in your app before handing over goods.

## What to Do If You Have Been Scammed

Act quickly:

* Immediately call your bank and request to block the transaction.

* Dial the National Cyber Crime Helpline 1930.

* File a complaint on the website cybercrime.gov.in.

* Change your UPI PIN away.

* Keep screenshots transaction details and messages as proof.

The you report, the better your chances of stopping the money from moving further.
